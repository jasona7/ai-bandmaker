Backstory

In the smoky haze of a London basement in 1974, Velvet Echoes was born, a sonic anomaly destined to etch its name into the annals of musical transcendence. Formed by four cosmic wanderers, each member brought a unique vibrational pulse that melded into an ethereal symphony of Psychedelic Rock and Ambient Electronica, a genre they would christen Sonic Dreamscapes. Frontman and spiritual guide, Jasper "Jaz" Nightshade, wielded his double-necked guitar like a shaman's staff. His riffs, drenched in reverb and delay, conjured visions of parallel universes. Nightshade's voice, a haunting baritone, narrated tales of astral voyages and existential reveries. Beside him, the enigmatic Seraphina "Sera" Moonlight commanded her Moog synthesizer with a sorceress's touch. Her labyrinthine soundscapes, layered with ghostly echoes and celestial drones, were the heartbeat of Velvet Echoes. Sera's fingers danced over keys, summoning aural spirits that whispered secrets of the cosmos. Percussionist and rhythmic alchemist, Thaddeus "Thad" Frost, was the grounding force. His drum kit, an assemblage of traditional and electronic elements, created a temporal vortex. Thad's beats, both primal and futuristic, injected the band's celestial musings with an urgent, earthly pulse. Completing the quartet, Orion "Oz" Zenith on bass guitar was the gravity that tethered their sound. His basslines, fluid and resonant, acted as the gravitational pull, drawing listeners deeper into Velvet Echoes' dreamscape. Oz's command of his instrument was nothing short of hypnotic, a magnetic force in a swirling nebula of sound.
